## Can Foreigners Buy Property in Florida?

Yes. International purchasers may lawfully acquire homes, condominiums, land, and investment real estate in Florida without citizenship or residency restrictions. Purchasable properties include primary residences, secondary dwellings, rental investments, commercial properties, and undeveloped land.

## Florida Foreign Real Estate Market Snapshot (2026)

According to the National Association of Realtors, foreign purchasers invested beyond $9 billion in Florida properties during the most recent reporting cycle. Florida maintains the leading position nationally for international acquisitions. Approximately 60% of foreign purchasers are visa holders or recent immigrants residing in Florida, while roughly 40% represent non-resident buyers acquiring from outside the state.

## Key Rules for Foreigners Buying Property in Florida

- No U.S. citizenship or permanent residency requirement exists
- Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) acquisition necessary if lacking Social Security Number
- Financing must originate from U.S.-based lending institutions
- Most lenders mandate 20 to 25% down payment for foreign nationals
- Tax obligations generally parallel those of U.S. citizens, contingent on property utilization

No constraints apply to acquiring primary residences, secondary properties, or investment real estate in Florida.

## Documents Foreign Buyers Need to Purchase Property

Lending institutions and title companies may request:

- Valid international passport
- Visa or entry documentation (if pertinent)
- ITIN or Social Security Number
- U.S. or foreign bank statements
- Proof of monetary resources and reserves
- Work or income verification
- U.S. or foreign tax returns (if applicable)
- Credit documentation (U.S. or international, when accessible)

Absence of U.S. credit history does not necessarily disqualify applicants from financing.

## How Do Foreigners Buy a House in Florida?

Six-step acquisition process:

- Select location based on lifestyle or investment purpose — our guide to the [cheapest places to buy a house in Florida](/learn/cheapest-places-to-buy-house-in-florida) can help

- Investigate properties through in-person or digital tours
- Research U.S. taxation, including rental income and resale regulations
- Submit proposal and negotiate conditions
- Arrange financing or remit funds in full, typically requiring 20 to 25% down payment for mortgaged purchases

Acquisition may proceed entirely remotely without Florida presence.

## Financing Options for Foreign Buyers in Florida

Foreign nationals possess financing alternatives, though mortgage availability differs from U.S. resident programs. Numerous lenders provide foreign national loan arrangements that forego U.S. credit requirements, SSN prerequisites, or permanent residency mandates.

Several foreign purchasers elect cash payment, while others leverage financing to maintain liquidity, diversify portfolios, or manage currency considerations.

## Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA)

FIRPTA regulations apply when international purchasers sell U.S. real property holdings, including Florida property. Purchasers commonly withhold 15% of gross transaction proceeds, forwarding it to the IRS as an advance toward the seller's probable U.S. capital gains taxation.

FIRPTA does not obstruct foreign acquisition in Florida but merits consideration for future disposition strategy. The mandatory withholding percentage may diminish or disappear through IRS certification, particularly for primary residency applications or reduced anticipated tax obligations.

Foreign purchasers regularly work with U.S. tax consultants to comprehend long-term tax consequences and compliance requirements.
