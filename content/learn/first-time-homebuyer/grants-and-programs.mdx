## Requirements for First-Time Home Buyer Grants & Programs

Understand that you, a first-time buyer in Florida, can access 105 grants and programs that may save you thousands of dollars buying your first home.

These include cash grants, tax credits, and reduced mortgage rates.

While each of these grants and programs have unique terms, the general qualifications include:

- **[Being a first-time home buyer](/learn/requirements-to-buy-a-house-in-florida)** according to the program's definition.
- Completing a home buyer education course.
- Adhering to specified [income and purchase price limits](/learn/florida-housing-income-purchase-price-limits).
- Purchasing a property in an approved area.
- Using the home as your primary residence.

## Florida Statewide Programs

These are the major statewide options most Florida homebuyers should check first before layering on any county or city assistance.

### Florida Hometown Heroes Loan Program

**Help:** Up to 5% of your loan amount (max $35,000) as a 0% interest second mortgage, forgiven after 5 years if you stay in the home. See our [Hometown Heroes marketing page](/hometown-heroes) for a full overview.

**Best For:** Teachers, nurses, law enforcement, military, and other eligible workers (see full job list). Check the [Hometown Heroes income limits by county](/learn/florida-hometown-heroes-income-limits) to confirm you qualify.

**Basics:** 640+ credit, income within county limits, must live in the home as your primary residence.

### Florida Assist Down Payment Loan (FL Assist)

**Help:** $10,000 at 0% interest with no monthly payments; repaid when you sell, refinance, or pay off your mortgage. Read our [full FL Assist breakdown](/learn/florida-assist-second-mortgage-program) for details on how this second mortgage works.

**Best For:** First-time buyers who want help at closing without raising their monthly payment.

**Basics:** 640+ credit, first-time homebuyer, must live in the home.

### Florida HLP Down Payment Loan (FL HLP)

**Help:** $12,500 second mortgage at 3% interest with a small monthly payment.

**Best For:** Buyers who need more than $10,000 and can handle an extra monthly payment.

**Basics:** 640+ credit, income within county limits, must live in the home as your primary residence.

### Florida HFA Preferred Plus Conventional Program

**Help:** 3%, 4%, or 5% of the loan amount as a repayable second mortgage with interest.

**Best For:** Conventional loan buyers who don't qualify for Hometown Heroes but still need help with down payment and closing costs.

**Basics:** 640+ credit, must meet income limits, must occupy the home.

### Salute Our Soldiers Military Loan Program

**Help:** Special fixed-rate first mortgage with access to down payment and closing cost assistance options for eligible military borrowers.

**Best For:** Active-duty service members, veterans, and certain surviving spouses buying a home in Florida.

**Basics:** Must meet military service eligibility, use the home as your primary residence, and qualify under program income, purchase price, and credit score guidelines.

### Florida HFA Preferred Grant (TBA) Program

**Help:** Provides 3–5% of the loan amount as a forgivable grant at 0% interest, forgiven after 5 years. Paired with a Fannie Mae HFA Preferred first mortgage for below-market rates.

**Best For:** First-time buyers who qualify for a Fannie Mae conventional loan and want a grant that disappears after 5 years.

**Basics:** Minimum 640 credit score. Must meet [Florida Housing income and purchase price limits](/learn/florida-housing-income-purchase-price-limits). Homebuyer education required. Available statewide through participating lenders.

### Florida HFA Advantage Plus Program

**Help:** Offers 3–5% of the loan amount as a forgivable grant at 0% interest, forgiven after 5 years. This is the Freddie Mac counterpart to the HFA Preferred program, paired with a Freddie Mac HFA Advantage first mortgage.

**Best For:** First-time buyers who qualify for a Freddie Mac conventional loan and want forgivable down payment help with no monthly second-mortgage payment.

**Basics:** Minimum 640 credit score. Must meet Florida Housing income and purchase price limits. Homebuyer education required. Available statewide through participating lenders.

## South Florida (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Monroe)

### Miami-Dade County Homebuyer Down Payment Assistance Program

**Help:** Interest-free, deferred-payment assistance (often up to around $35,000) to cover part of your down payment and closing costs.

**Best For:** First-time buyers with steady income but limited savings who want to buy anywhere in Miami-Dade County.

**Basics:** Must meet county income and purchase-price limits, be a first-time homebuyer, use an approved first mortgage, and live in the home as your primary residence.

### Miami-Dade Advocacy Trust Grant ($28,500)

**Help:** Up to $28,500 for down payment and closing costs as a 0% interest, no-payment second mortgage that's fully forgiven after 20 years if you stay in the home and follow program rules.

**Best For:** Miami-Dade buyers under local income limits who need a big chunk of upfront help and plan to own long-term.

**Basics:** Must be a first-time homebuyer, meet income and purchase-price caps, buy in Miami-Dade County, complete homebuyer education, and occupy the home as your primary residence.

### Miami-Dade HFA First-Time Homebuyer Program

**Help:** Down payment help bundled with a Miami-Dade Housing Finance Authority first mortgage—typically a percentage of your loan amount in assistance that doesn't have to be repaid if guidelines are met.

**Best For:** First-time buyers who want a 30-year fixed HFA loan and like built-in assistance instead of a separate second mortgage.

**Basics:** Must meet HFA income, credit, and purchase-price guidelines, work with an approved lender, buy in Miami-Dade County, and live in the home as your primary residence.

### City of North Miami First-Time Homebuyer Program

**Help:** Up to $80,000 in layered assistance — $40,000 in SHIP funds forgiven after 7 years plus $40,000 in HOME funds forgiven after 15 years. One of the most generous city-level programs in Miami-Dade.

**Best For:** First-time buyers purchasing in North Miami who have limited savings and can complete the required homebuyer education.

**Basics:** Maximum purchase price $460,000. Maximum liquid assets $30,000. Must complete 8-hour HUD-approved homebuyer education course. Income limits apply.

### City of Miami Beach First-Time Homebuyer Program

**Help:** Between $40,000 and $150,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance, depending on household income. The loan is forgivable after 15 years of owner occupancy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<div className="faq-section">

<details>
<summary>What is a first-time home buyer in Florida?</summary>
<div className="faq-answer">In Florida, most assistance programs define a first-time home buyer as someone who has not owned a home in the past three years. This includes individuals who have never owned a home, as well as those who previously owned but have been renting for at least three years. Some programs also include displaced homemakers and single parents who only owned with a former spouse.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>How much down payment assistance can I get in Florida?</summary>
<div className="faq-answer">Assistance amounts vary widely by program and location. Statewide programs like Hometown Heroes offer up to $35,000, while some local programs offer dramatically more — Boca Raton offers up to $190,000, Seminole County up to $145,000, and Nassau County up to $102,747 in deferred, zero-interest assistance for eligible buyers.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Do I have to pay back down payment assistance in Florida?</summary>
<div className="faq-answer">It depends on the program. Many Florida programs offer forgivable loans that are fully forgiven after 5–20 years of continuous owner-occupancy. Others are deferred loans that only become due when you sell, refinance, or move out. A few are true grants that never need to be repaid.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>What credit score do I need for Florida home buyer programs?</summary>
<div className="faq-answer">Most statewide programs require a minimum credit score of 640. Some local programs accept scores as low as 620. A higher credit score can qualify you for more programs and better terms.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I combine multiple Florida home buyer programs?</summary>
<div className="faq-answer">Yes, many Florida buyers stack statewide and local programs together. For example, you might use a Florida Housing first mortgage with the Hometown Heroes down payment assistance and also receive a local city or county grant — potentially combining tens of thousands of dollars in total assistance.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Do I need to take a homebuyer education course?</summary>
<div className="faq-answer">Almost every Florida down payment assistance program requires completion of a HUD-approved homebuyer education course. These courses are typically 6–8 hours and cover budgeting, the mortgage process, and homeowner responsibilities. Many are available online for free or at low cost.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Are there income limits for Florida home buyer programs?</summary>
<div className="faq-answer">Yes, nearly all programs have income limits based on your household size and the county where you are buying. Limits are typically tied to the Area Median Income (AMI) and vary by program — some cap eligibility at 80% AMI, while others go up to 140% AMI. See our [2026 Florida Housing income and purchase price limits by county](/learn/florida-housing-income-purchase-price-limits) for the full breakdown.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>How long does it take to get approved for down payment assistance?</summary>
<div className="faq-answer">The timeline varies by program. Some statewide programs process approvals within a few weeks through participating lenders. Local city and county programs may take 30–90 days depending on funding availability, documentation requirements, and application volume.</div>
</details>
