## What Is the Florida Good Neighbor Next Door Program?

The HUD Good Neighbor Next Door (GNND) Program is a federal housing initiative that helps stabilize revitalization areas by encouraging public servants to live where they work.

**In Florida, eligible buyers can:**

- Purchase HUD-owned homes at 50% off the list price
- Use FHA financing with just $100 down
- Have the 50% discount forgiven after 36 months of occupancy

**Homes must be:**

- Located in HUD-designated revitalization areas
- Listed exclusively on the HUD Home Store
- Owner-occupied as a primary residence

## Good Neighbor Next Door Program Eligibility (Florida)

To qualify for the FHA Good Neighbor Next Door (GNND) Program in Florida, buyers must meet both professional and occupancy requirements set by HUD.

This program is limited and highly specific, so confirming eligibility early is critical.

**General Requirements**

- Are currently employed full-time as a K–12 teacher, law enforcement officer, firefighter, or Emergency Medical Technician (EMT)
- Purchase a HUD-owned home located in a designated GNND revitalization area in Florida
- Agree to use the home as your sole primary residence
- Commit to 36 months of continuous owner-occupancy
- Qualify for FHA financing, including credit, income, and property requirements
- Are not already homeowners at the time of purchase (first-time buyer status is typically required)

**Important Florida Considerations**

- GNND homes are limited in supply and may not be available in every Florida county or city
- Buyers must usually move in within 60 days of closing
- Renting, Airbnb use, or second-home use during the 36-month period is not allowed
- Failure to meet occupancy requirements can trigger repayment of the silent second mortgage

If you serve your Florida community as a teacher, first responder, or EMT — and are ready to live in the home for at least three years — the GNND program can offer one of the largest homebuyer discounts available.

## How the Florida GNND Program Works (Step-by-Step)

A simple step-by-step breakdown showing how buyers apply, enter the HUD lottery, finance the home, and earn full forgiveness of the 50% discount after three years.

- Search HUD-approved GNND homes in Florida
- Submit an offer during the 7-day GNND lottery
- Get selected (if multiple buyers apply, HUD runs a lottery)
- Finance with FHA ($100 down) or approved alternatives
- Sign a second "silent" mortgage equal to the 50% discount
- Live in the home for 36 months
- Discount is fully forgiven after 3 years

## How the Silent Second Mortgage Works

An explanation of the 50% discount structure, how the no-payment second mortgage functions, and when it's forgiven.

- **What it is:** A 0% interest, no-payment second mortgage equal to the 50% discount.
- **Payments:** None, as long as you comply.
- **Forgiveness:** The second mortgage is fully forgiven after 36 months of continuous owner-occupancy.
- **If you leave early:** Selling, renting, or moving out before 36 months triggers repayment of the remaining unforgiven balance (prorated).
