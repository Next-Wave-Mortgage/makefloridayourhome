## Florida Manufactured Home Loan Program Overview

Florida manufactured home loan programs help buyers purchase HUD-compliant manufactured homes using government-backed or approved conventional financing.

These programs support affordable housing by offering:

- Lower down payments
- Flexible credit requirements
- Long-term fixed-rate financing

The Florida Manufactured Home Loan Program exists to address rising housing costs and expand access to homeownership for low- and moderate-income households.

By allowing manufactured homes to qualify for traditional mortgage financing, these programs:

- Reduce monthly housing costs
- Expand homeownership opportunities statewide
- Support both rural and suburban communities

Manufactured homes must meet HUD construction standards and be properly titled and affixed to qualify for most mortgage programs.

## Manufactured Home Loan Options in Florida

Manufactured home loans in Florida allow buyers to finance homes built after June 15, 1976, using FHA, VA, USDA, conventional, or chattel loans.

The right option depends on whether you own the land, how the home is titled, and whether it's permanently affixed. Understanding Florida's land and title rules is key to qualifying.

### FHA Manufactured Home Loans

Best for first-time buyers who own land or are buying land with the home. See our [FHA eligibility guide](/learn/fha-loan-eligibility-requirements-florida) for full qualification details.

- For primary residences
- Low down payment (as little as 3.5%)
- Home must be permanently affixed and titled as real property
- Land must be owned or purchased with the home

### VA Manufactured Home Loans

Best for veterans buying on owned land or combined land + home purchase.

- For eligible veterans and service members
- 0% down possible
- Home must be permanently affixed and meet VA property standards
- Land ownership required (or included in the purchase)

### USDA Manufactured Home Loans

Best for rural Florida buyers meeting [USDA income and location rules](/learn/usda-loans-florida).

- For rural areas only
- 0% down option
- [Income limits](/learn/florida-housing-income-purchase-price-limits) apply
- Home must be permanently affixed and titled as real estate

### Chattel (Personal Property) Loans

Best for buyers purchasing a home in a park without land ownership.

- Used when the home is not permanently affixed
- Common in manufactured home parks
- Higher interest rates and shorter terms
- Home is titled as personal property, not real estate

## Florida Land-Ownership & Titling Rules

In Florida, how your manufactured home is classified and titled directly impacts loan eligibility:

**Real Property (Preferred)**

- Home is permanently affixed to a foundation
- Land is owned by the borrower
- Title is retired and recorded with the county
- Eligible for FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional loans

**Personal Property (Chattel)**

- Home is not permanently affixed
- Land is leased (park or community)
- Titled like a vehicle
- Not eligible for FHA, VA, or USDA mortgages

Converting a manufactured home to real property may allow refinancing into lower-rate mortgage programs.

## How to Apply for a Manufactured Home Loan in Florida

A step-by-step overview of the application and approval process.

- Gather income, asset, and credit documents
- Choose an eligible loan program
- Get pre-approved
- Select a HUD-compliant manufactured home
- Complete appraisal, inspection, and underwriting
- Close and move in

**Required Documentation**

- Government-issued photo ID
- Proof of income (pay stubs, W-2s, tax returns)
- Credit report authorization
- Asset documentation
- Manufactured home details (HUD tags, foundation info)

**Tips for a Successful Application**

- Review your credit before applying
- Resolve title or foundation issues early
- Use a lender experienced with manufactured homes
- Consider down payment assistance options
