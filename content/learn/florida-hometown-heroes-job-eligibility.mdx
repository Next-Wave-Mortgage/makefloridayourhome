The [Florida Hometown Heroes Program](/hometown-heroes) gives Florida's frontline workforce up to $35,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance — but the eligibility test surprises people. It's not really about your job title.

**Eligibility is determined by your employer and where you physically work.** A janitor at a K-12 school qualifies. A cafeteria worker at a hospital qualifies. A cashier at a retail pharmacy doesn't — because a retail store isn't an eligible healthcare employer, no matter how healthcare-adjacent the shelves look. And only one borrower on the loan needs to pass the test.

This guide covers the eligible employer categories, the occupation examples, the rules that disqualify otherwise-perfect applicants, and the documentation your lender will need.

## The Four-Question Test

Lenders evaluate Hometown Heroes employment eligibility with four questions:

1. **Who employs you?** The employer must be an eligible Florida-based type (listed below).
2. **Where do you report to work?** There must be a physical, brick-and-mortar Florida location you work from.
3. **Who pays you?** W-2 employment, or a single qualifying 1099 arrangement.
4. **Can it be documented?** Paystubs, a verification of employment, or an employer letter sent from a work email.

Alongside the employment test, you must be **currently employed full-time** (offer letters don't count), a first-time homebuyer (veterans and targeted-area buyers exempt), buying a Florida primary residence, within your [county's income limits](/learn/florida-hometown-heroes-income-limits), and have a credit score of 640 or higher.

## Eligible Employers and Occupations

The occupation lists below are official examples — they're explicitly "not limited to" these titles. If your employer fits the category and you work there full-time at a Florida location, you likely qualify.

### Healthcare Workers

*Eligible employers: Florida-based healthcare providers and physicians, hospitals, assisted living facilities, mental health facilities, and other healthcare facilities.*

**Nursing:** Advanced Practice Registered Nurse (APRN), Clinical Nurse Specialist, Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN), Nurse Anesthetist, Nurse Midwife, Nursing Assistant (CNA), Nursing Technician, Registered Nurse (RN). Nurses may also want to compare the [Florida Nurse Next Door Program](/learn/florida-nurse-next-door-program).

**Medical technicians:** Anesthesia Support, Cardiology, Clinical, CT, ER, Laboratory (including Phlebotomist), Music, Neurology, Nuclear Medicine, Monitor, Orthopedic, Pharmacy, Radiation, Rehabilitation, Surgical, and X-ray Technicians, plus Sonographers.

**Medical therapists:** Audiology, Occupational, Physical, Respiratory, and Speech Therapists.

**Mental health workers:** Behavioral Health Specialists, Technicians, and Therapists; Case Managers; Mental Health Specialists, Technicians, and Therapists; Psychiatrists; Psychologists; Social Workers.

**Doctors, physicians, and pharmacists:** Anesthesiologists and Anesthesiologist Assistants, Dentists, Nuclear Pharmacists, Pharmacists, Pharmacy Technicians, Physicians, Physician Assistants.

**Other healthcare workers:** Dental Assistants and Hygienists, Medical Assistants (CMA/MA), Patient Care Assistants, Patient Transporters (Orderlies), Phlebotomists.

### School Staff Members

*Eligible employers: Florida-based K-12 public, private, charter, and magnet schools.*

Assistant Principal, Career Specialist (Guidance Counselor), Classroom Teacher, Food Service / Cafeteria Staff, Librarian / Media Specialist, Maintenance / Janitorial Staff, Principal, School Counselor, School Psychologist, Social Worker, Teacher Aide.

Note the K-12 requirement: college and university employees don't qualify — including instructors teaching high-school students in dual-enrollment college courses.

### First Responders

*Eligible employers: Florida-based fire departments, ambulance services, and hospitals.*

Certified Emergency Medical Technician (EMT), Certified Paramedic, Firefighter.

First responders should also review [Florida first responder homebuyer grants and programs](/learn/florida-first-responder-homebuyer-grants-programs) for assistance that can complement or substitute for Hometown Heroes.

### Public Safety Workers

*Eligible employers: Florida-based local, county, state, or federal law enforcement departments, juvenile detention facilities, correctional facilities, and emergency operations centers.*

911 Public Safety Communicator (Operator / Dispatcher), Correctional Officer, Correctional Probation Officer, Juvenile Detention Officer, Juvenile Probation Officer, Sworn Law Enforcement Officer (local, state, or federal).

### Court Employees

*Eligible employers: Florida-based county, state, or federal courts, court administrators' offices, State Attorney's Offices, and Public Defender's Offices.*

Assistant State Attorney, Assistant Public Defender, Court Clerk, and Attorneys and Court Stenographers/Reporters employed by the Florida Supreme Court, District Courts of Appeal, Circuit Courts, or county courts.

### Childcare Workers

*Eligible employers: Florida-based licensed child care facilities, and home day cares that are licensed, registered, or legally exempt from licensure.*

Any worker at a qualifying facility is eligible — teachers, aides, and support staff alike.

### Military and Veterans

**Active-duty and reserve service members** of every U.S. Armed Forces branch qualify, including the Florida National Guard. A current Leave and Earnings Statement (LES) is required.

**Veterans** follow a different rule: with an other-than-dishonorable discharge (documented by a DD-214), a veteran can work full-time for *any* Florida-based employer — the eligible-employer categories don't apply. Veterans are also exempt from the first-time homebuyer requirement. Full-time employment is still required, so retired veterans without a job don't qualify.

One honest caveat: because the assistance must be repaid when you sell or stop living in the home, service members who expect to relocate within a few years should weigh whether Hometown Heroes is the right fit — a [VA loan](/check-va-loan-eligibility) alone may serve them better.

## The Remote Work Rule

This is the most common reason otherwise-qualified buyers get denied:

- **Fully remote employees are not eligible.** It doesn't matter if the employer is a Florida hospital system — if you work from home full-time, the file will be denied.
- **Hybrid employees can qualify** only if they report to their employer's Florida brick-and-mortar location **at least 3 days per week**.
- The work location must be a **specific physical Florida address**. A city name, a Google search result, or a home, virtual, or registered-agent address doesn't satisfy the requirement.

## Who on the Loan Must Qualify

- **Only one occupying borrower** needs to meet the employment requirement.
- **Co-borrowers** without qualifying jobs are fine — but their income counts toward [program income limits](/learn/florida-hometown-heroes-income-limits).
- **Non-occupying co-signors cannot** satisfy the employment requirement.
- **Non-occupying co-borrowers are prohibited** from the program entirely.

## Self-Employed and 1099 Workers

Self-employed borrowers can qualify, but the rules are strict:

- You must work **full-time for one 1099 employer** — multiple part-time 1099 gigs can't be combined.
- That employer (or your business, if paid directly) must have a **physical Florida location** where the work happens.
- Documentation requires a **CPA, bookkeeper, or tax preparer letter** confirming full-time self-employment (your own letter of explanation isn't acceptable), your most recent signed federal tax return, and paperwork tying the work to the Florida address.
- Qualifying income is **net** — after business expenses — and negative business income counts as zero.

If you can't tie your work to one employer and one Florida location, the program won't work for you — but a [Non-QM loan](/check-non-qm-loan-eligibility) might.

## Common Disqualifiers

Real scenarios that get files denied:

- Nurses working remotely for insurance companies or telehealth platforms
- Hospital **corporate-office** employees — even on the same campus as the hospital
- Behavioral therapists and occupational therapists working **multiple sites** with no single qualifying employer
- Home health aides **paid in cash** with no 1099
- School bus drivers who report to a **central bus depot** rather than one school
- Social workers assigned to **multiple schools** instead of one K-12 location
- College and university instructors (K-12 only)
- Retail workers at healthcare-adjacent businesses, like pharmacy cashiers
- Retired veterans with no current employment
- **Offer letters** — employment must be current at closing, not starting soon

## The Documentation Your Lender Needs

- **Proof of full-time work:** paystubs showing 40 hours, a written or verbal verification of employment, or an employer letter — sent from the employer's **work email** with a full signature block, not a personal address.
- **Proof of the Florida work location:** a VOE or paystub showing a specific physical Florida address.
- **The Hometown Heroes Borrower Certification form,** signed for every file.
- **Self-employed borrowers:** the CPA/tax-preparer letter and tax returns described above.

A participating Hometown Heroes lender will assemble this with you — employment documentation is where inexperienced lenders lose files, so it pays to work with one who closes these loans regularly.

## The Bottom Line

Don't rule yourself out because your exact title isn't on a list — and don't assume you're in because it is. Ask the four questions: who employs you, where do you physically work, who pays you, and can you document it? If the answers point to full-time work at an eligible Florida employer's physical location, you're likely eligible for up to $35,000 in assistance.

Occupation is one requirement of several — [income limits](/learn/florida-hometown-heroes-income-limits), purchase price caps, first-time buyer status, and credit still apply. For the complete landscape of alternatives, see the [Florida first-time homebuyer grants and programs guide](/learn/first-time-homebuyer/grants-and-programs).
