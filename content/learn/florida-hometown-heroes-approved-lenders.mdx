The [Florida Hometown Heroes Program](/hometown-heroes) helps frontline workers, veterans, and first-time homebuyers afford homeownership through up to $35,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance.

But there is one rule that matters more than almost any other: you must work with a Florida Housing-approved lender to access Hometown Heroes funds.

## What Is the Florida Hometown Heroes Program?

The Florida Hometown Heroes Program is a state-run homebuyer assistance initiative administered by Florida Housing to support eligible full-time workers and veterans purchasing homes in their communities.

**Key Program Highlights (2026)**

- Up to $35,000 (or 5% of the first mortgage) in assistance
- Assistance provided as a 0% interest, deferred second mortgage
- No monthly payments
- Repayment only upon sale, refinance, or payoff of the first mortgage
- Compatible with FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional loans
- Exemption from Florida documentary stamp and intangible taxes, reducing closing costs

**Core Eligibility Rules**

- First-time homebuyer (veterans exempt)
- Minimum 640 credit score
- Household income at or below 150% of county AMI ([see income limits by county](/learn/florida-hometown-heroes-income-limits))
- Full-time employment in an approved occupation ([see job eligibility](/learn/florida-hometown-heroes-job-eligibility))
- Purchase in the same Florida community where you work

## Why You Must Use a Florida Hometown Heroes Approved Lender

Not every lender in Florida is authorized to offer Hometown Heroes loans. This is one of the most common — and costly — mistakes buyers make.

A lender cannot "add" Hometown Heroes later. A lender cannot "submit it for you" if they are not approved. A lender cannot transfer the benefit to another lender mid-process.

If your lender is not officially approved by Florida Housing, you cannot receive Hometown Heroes funds, regardless of what is promised verbally.

## Are There "Top" Florida Hometown Heroes Lenders?

Florida Housing does not rank or endorse lenders publicly. However, strong participating lenders typically share these traits:

- Active Florida Housing approval
- Dedicated program knowledge
- Proven experience with funding windows and pauses
- Clear communication about eligibility and timelines

Rather than chasing a "top 5 list," buyers are best served by working with a verified approved lender and a loan officer who regularly closes Hometown Heroes loans.

## How to Choose the Right Florida Hometown Heroes Lender

Not all approved Hometown Heroes lenders operate the same way. Choosing the right lender can impact approval speed, rate, grant availability, and overall experience.

**Confirm Active Hometown Heroes Approval**

Make sure the lender is currently approved by Florida Housing and actively originating Hometown Heroes loans. Some lenders appear on lists but are not accepting new Hometown Heroes applications.

**Ask About Program Availability and Funding Windows**

Hometown Heroes funding is limited and time-sensitive. Choose a lender that monitors funding releases closely, can lock loans quickly when funds open, and has experience submitting files during high-demand periods.

**Verify Down Payment and Closing Cost Assistance Options**

Ask if they can combine Hometown Heroes with other Florida assistance programs such as the [Florida Assist second mortgage program](/learn/florida-assist-second-mortgage-program). Some lenders offer only the base program while others layer additional assistance through local grants or employer programs.

**Experience With First-Time Buyers**

Hometown Heroes is designed for first-time and moderate-income buyers. Look for lenders who explain income calculations clearly, help document job eligibility properly, and guide you through homebuyer education requirements.

**Turn Times and Communication**

Fast, clear communication matters — especially in competitive markets. Ask about average pre-approval turnaround time, underwriting and closing timelines, and who your main point of contact will be.

**Local Florida Knowledge**

Florida-specific experience matters. A strong Hometown Heroes lender understands county income limits, condo approval rules, insurance and flood zone issues, and property tax and homestead exemptions.
