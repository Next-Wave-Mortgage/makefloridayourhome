## What Are Down Payment Assistance (DPA) Programs?

Down Payment Assistance (DPA) programs help cover the upfront costs of buying a home, including:

- Down payments
- Closing costs
- Prepaid taxes and insurance

For single moms, DPAs are often the difference between renting and owning, because they reduce or eliminate the need for large savings.

Most Florida DPA programs are offered through:

- State housing agencies
- County and city housing departments
- Approved lenders

Many programs do not require repayment if you meet occupancy rules.

## Types of Down Payment Assistance Available to Single Moms

Florida single moms can access four primary types of assistance, each with different benefits depending on your income, credit, and long-term plans.

### Grants (Free Money)

Grants are the most powerful form of assistance because they never have to be repaid.

- Funded by state, county, or city housing programs
- Ideal for reducing cash-to-close
- Do not increase monthly payments

**Estimated Benefit:** Up to $10,000+, depending on location and funding availability

### Forgivable Loans

Forgivable loans start as a loan — but are forgiven over time (typically 5–10 years).

- No monthly payments
- Forgiven if you remain in the home
- Often used by counties and cities

**Estimated Benefit:** Up to $15,000–$35,000

### Deferred Payment Loans

Deferred loans require no payments until a future event, such as:

- Selling the home
- Refinancing
- Moving out

These are common in Florida Housing programs and allow single moms to buy now without increasing monthly expenses.

**Estimated Benefit:** Up to $12,000–$40,000, depending on the program

### Low-Interest Second Mortgages

Some programs offer low-interest loans (often 0–3%) to assist with upfront costs.

- Monthly payment may apply
- Often paired with grants or FHA loans
- Lower long-term cost than traditional borrowing

**Estimated Benefit:** $5,000–$8,000 over the life of the loan

## Best Florida Homebuyer Programs for Single Moms (2026)

### Florida Hometown Heroes Housing Program

One of the most powerful statewide programs for working single moms.

**Key Benefits:**

- Up to 5% of the loan amount (max $35,000)
- 0% interest, 30-year deferred second mortgage
- No monthly payments
- Below-market first-mortgage rates

**Who Qualifies:**

- Full-time Florida workers (most professions qualify in 2026)
- First-time homebuyer
- Minimum 640 FICO
- Income limits apply by county

Many single moms qualify simply by being employed full-time in Florida.

A strong county-level option in select areas.

### FHA Good Neighbor Next Door (GNND)

For single moms in public service professions.

**Major Benefit:**

- 50% off the home's list price
- $100 FHA down payment option
- Silent second mortgage forgiven after 3 years

**Eligible Professions:**

- Teachers
- Firefighters
- Law enforcement officers
- EMTs

Homes must be in HUD-designated revitalization areas.

## Loan Options Compared: What Works Best for Single Moms in Florida

A side-by-side look at FHA, USDA, VA, and conventional loans to help single moms choose the mortgage option that best fits their income, credit, and location.

| Loan Type | Down Payment | Credit Flexibility | Best For |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|----------|

## Requirements for Single Moms Using Homebuyer Assistance

While each program has unique rules, most Florida programs share these baseline requirements:

- **Income & Purchase Price Limits:** Limits vary by county and household size. Targeted areas may allow higher limits. See the latest [Florida housing income and purchase price limits](/learn/florida-housing-income-purchase-price-limits).
- **Primary Residence Requirement:** Must live in the home full-time. No rentals or investment properties.
- **Approved Property Location:** Some programs restrict eligible neighborhoods or counties.
- **Credit & Employment:** Credit minimums typically range 580–660. Stable employment required.

Being a single parent does not disqualify you — income, not marital status, is what matters. If you're exploring other paths to homeownership, [rent-to-own programs in Florida](/learn/florida-rent-to-own-programs) can be another viable option.
