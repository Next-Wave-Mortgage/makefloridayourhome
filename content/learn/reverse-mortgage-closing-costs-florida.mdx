Reverse mortgages — formally called Home Equity Conversion Mortgages (HECMs) — allow Florida homeowners age 62 and older to convert home equity into cash without monthly mortgage payments.

With U.S. homeowners holding more than $11 trillion in home equity, reverse mortgages have become an increasingly popular retirement strategy for Florida seniors looking to supplement income, eliminate an existing mortgage, or establish a growing line of credit.

However, before moving forward, it is critical to understand reverse mortgage closing costs in Florida, how much they are, and how they are paid.

This guide breaks down every fee, explains Florida-specific taxes, and shows you what to expect in 2026.

## How Reverse Mortgages Work in Florida

A reverse mortgage allows eligible homeowners to access 40% to 80% of their home's value, depending on:

- Age of the youngest borrower
- Current interest rates
- Home value
- FHA lending limits

Instead of making monthly payments, the loan balance increases over time and is typically repaid when the home is sold, the borrower moves out, or passes away.

## Reverse Mortgage Closing Costs in Florida (Detailed Breakdown)

Florida reverse mortgage closing costs include FHA insurance, lender origination fees, third-party services, and state taxes — most of which are added to the loan balance rather than paid upfront.

**Origination Fee (FHA-Capped)**

The lender's fee for processing the loan. How it is calculated:

- 2% of the first $200,000 of home value
- 1% of the amount above $200,000
- Maximum: $6,000

**Initial Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP)**

Provides FHA insurance that protects borrowers if the loan balance exceeds the home value. Cost equals 2% of the lower of the home's appraised value or the FHA HECM lending limit ($1,149,825).

**Appraisal Fee**

Determines property value and FHA eligibility.

- Typical range: $400 to $600
- May be higher for rural, large, or unique properties

**HUD Counseling Fee (Required)**

Independent counseling ensures borrowers understand the loan terms.

- Cost: $125 to $200
- Usually the only out-of-pocket cost

**Third-Party Fees (Typical Florida Costs)**

| Fee | Estimated Cost |

| Title insurance | $1,000 to $2,500 |
| Flood certification | Approximately $15 |
| Credit report | $60 to $106 |

## Florida-Specific Reverse Mortgage Taxes

Florida has two unique taxes that apply to reverse mortgages:

- **Documentary Stamp Tax:** $0.35 per $100 of loan amount
- **Intangible Tax:** 0.2% of the loan amount

## Total Reverse Mortgage Closing Costs in Florida (2026)

Most Florida reverse mortgage closing costs fall between $9,000 to $35,000. Most borrowers finance these costs into the loan, reducing upfront cash needs.
