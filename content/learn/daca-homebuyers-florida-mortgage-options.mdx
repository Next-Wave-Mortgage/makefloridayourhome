In 2025, FHA updated its guidance to restrict eligibility for DACA recipients, meaning borrowers without certain qualifying lawful residency statuses can no longer use FHA-insured loans.

While this change removes FHA as an option, it does not eliminate homeownership pathways in Florida — many buyers can still qualify through conventional, state housing, local assistance, portfolio, and alternative programs that do not rely on FHA insurance. For background on what FHA previously offered, see our [FHA eligibility guide](/learn/fha-loan-eligibility-requirements-florida).

## The 2025–2026 FHA Policy Change (What Actually Happened)

In HUD Title I Letter 490, FHA revised its borrower eligibility rules:

- FHA loans are limited to U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents
- DACA recipients are no longer eligible
- Lenders must verify permanent residency via USCIS documentation

This applies nationwide, including all Florida FHA loans.

## Why FHA Loans Were Popular With Florida DACA Buyers

Before the rule change, [FHA loans](/home-loan/fha-loan) were the most DACA-friendly option in Florida because they offered:

- 3.5% down payments
- Flexible credit standards
- Manual underwriting options
- Broad lender availability

That path is now closed — but Florida buyers still have alternatives.

## 10 Better Options for Florida Homebuyers (When FHA Isn't Available)

Even though FHA loans are no longer available to DACA recipients, other financing paths remain open. Here are a few alternatives to explore:

Program rules can change, and eligibility depends on lender policies and documentation.

### Conventional Loans (Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac)

Many Florida buyers qualify for conventional financing with competitive rates and down payments as low as 3% (program-dependent). These loans do not use FHA insurance and are widely available through banks and mortgage brokers.

**Why it helps:** No FHA MIP; broader lender participation.

### Florida Housing First-Time Buyer Programs

State programs (e.g., HFA Preferred / HFA Advantage) can pair conventional loans with down-payment and closing-cost assistance for eligible buyers. See our [complete guide to Florida grants and programs](/learn/first-time-homebuyer/grants-and-programs) for details.

**Why it helps:** Reduces upfront cash even without FHA.

### County & City Down-Payment Assistance (DPA)

Local programs across Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Orange, Hillsborough, Duval, and others offer grants or deferred loans layered with conventional financing.

**Why it helps:** Local funds can bridge the down-payment gap.

### Portfolio Loans From Florida Lenders

Some Florida banks and credit unions offer portfolio loans (kept in-house) with flexible guidelines.

**Why it helps:** Lender discretion allows alternatives to agency rules.

### Community Development & Employer Programs

Certain community housing organizations and employer-assisted housing programs support workforce buyers with forgivable or deferred assistance.

**Why it helps:** Tied to employment or community eligibility, not FHA.

### ITIN-Based Mortgage Programs

Select Florida lenders offer ITIN loans for borrowers without an SSN, subject to income verification and lender criteria.

**Why it helps:** A path when traditional documentation differs.

### Credit-Union Programs

Florida credit unions sometimes provide member-only mortgages with flexible terms and lower fees.

**Why it helps:** Relationship-based underwriting and local focus.

### Co-Borrower / Non-Occupant Co-Borrower Structures

Adding a qualified co-borrower (where allowed) can strengthen income and credit to meet conventional guidelines.

**Why it helps:** Improves approval odds without FHA.

### Seller Credits & Rate Buydowns

Negotiating seller concessions or temporary rate buydowns can reduce upfront and monthly costs on non-FHA loans.

**Why it helps:** Offsets affordability without FHA assistance.

### Waiting + Credit Optimization Strategy

For buyers close to qualifying, a short credit improvement plan (paydowns, corrections) can unlock conventional approval.

**Why it helps:** Converts near-misses into approvals quickly.

## Florida DACA Mortgage Requirements (2026)

This section outlines the core documentation and underwriting expectations Florida lenders use when reviewing mortgage applications from DACA recipients in 2026.

While FHA loans are no longer available, meeting these requirements can help you qualify for conventional, ITIN, or portfolio loan programs still offered across Florida.

| Requirement | Florida Lender Expectation |
|---|---|
| EAD (C33) | Must be valid and renewable; lenders often review renewal history |
| SSN or ITIN | Required to complete underwriting and tax documentation |
| Credit Score | 620+ preferred for conventional loans; some portfolio lenders allow flexibility |
| Income | Verifiable, stable income with consistent employment history |
| Debt-to-Income (DTI) | Ideally under 43%, though higher may be allowed with compensating factors |
| Occupancy | Must be a primary residence (no investment or vacation properties) |
| Lender Experience | Critical — lender must be familiar with DACA and non-permanent resident guidelines |

## What Florida DACA Buyers Should Look For in a Loan

When choosing a mortgage as a DACA recipient, here are key factors to consider:

- **Residency Requirements:** Does the lender accept DACA status or require permanent residency?
- **Down Payment:** Look for low down payment options like 3–5% or grants that cover it.
- **Credit Flexibility:** Some programs allow non-traditional credit history or lower scores.
- **Mortgage Insurance Costs:** Consider PMI or alternative insurance fees that affect your monthly payment.
- **Documentation Needed:** Confirm what proof of work status or income is required (EAD, ITIN, etc.).

By focusing on these factors, you can find a program that fits your financial situation and legal status.
