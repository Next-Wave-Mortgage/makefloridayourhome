The Florida Nurse Next Door Program is a specialized homebuyer assistance initiative created to help nurses and healthcare professionals overcome the financial barriers to homeownership.

In 2026, eligible Florida healthcare workers may access up to $15,000 in total benefits, including non-repayable grants, down payment assistance, and professional guidance throughout the homebuying process.

Unlike traditional loan programs, Nurse Next Door focuses on occupation-based eligibility, not income caps alone — making it especially valuable for nurses who earn solid incomes but still struggle with upfront costs in Florida's competitive housing market.

The assistance is typically delivered through down payment help, closing cost assistance, and lender credits, not a direct cash grant, and is paired with FHA, VA, USDA, or conventional loans.

## Overview of the Nurse Next Door Program

The Nurse Next Door Program is part of a national initiative supporting nurses and healthcare professionals across all 50 states, including Florida.

Rather than being a single government loan, the program works by layering multiple benefits, which may include:

- National housing grants
- State and local down payment assistance
- FHA, VA, USDA, or conventional mortgage programs
- Credit optimization and counseling

In Florida, this approach allows nurses to combine assistance programs to significantly reduce out-of-pocket costs at closing.

## How the Nurse Next Door Program Works

A step-by-step guided process that helps nurses move from eligibility to closing with professional support.

- **Apply Online** — Complete a short eligibility form (no credit check required)
- **Program Review** — A specialist reviews available grants and assistance options
- **Mortgage Pre-Approval** — Get matched with loan options that fit your profile
- **Home Search** — Work with a licensed Florida Realtor partner
- **Closing Support** — Assistance is applied at closing to reduce upfront costs

The program acts as a navigator, helping nurses access benefits they may not find on their own.

## Eligibility Requirements for Florida Nurses

To participate in the Florida Nurse Next Door Program in 2026, you must:

- Work as a nurse or healthcare professional (clinical or support role)
- Provide proof of stable healthcare employment
- Purchase a home used as your primary residence
- Qualify for a mortgage through an approved lender

First-time buyers are encouraged, but prior homeownership does not automatically disqualify you. There are no national income limits, though local assistance programs may impose caps. Credit requirements depend on the mortgage program used (FHA, VA, conventional, etc.).

## Florida Nurse Next Door vs Traditional Down Payment Assistance

Unlike many traditional down payment assistance programs that restrict eligibility based strictly on income, Nurse Next Door:

- Focuses on profession, not salary
- Allows program stacking
- Provides hands-on guidance, not just funding

This makes it especially powerful for Florida nurses who earn good incomes but still face affordability challenges.

| Program | Administered By | Typical Benefit | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| Nurse Next Door | National program network | Up to ~$15,000 (combined benefits) | Not a Florida-run program |
| Florida Hometown Heroes | Florida Housing | State down payment assistance (funding cycles apply) | Income & purchase price limits apply |
| County / City Nurse Programs | Local governments | Varies (often larger assistance amounts) | Typically forgivable or deferred loans |
