The Orange County Down Payment Assistance Program helps eligible Florida homebuyers with up to $70,000 toward down payment and closing costs.

Assistance is typically structured as a deferred or forgivable loan and paired with FHA, VA, USDA, or conventional financing for primary residences in Orange County.

This program helps cover down payment and closing costs, reducing the biggest barrier to homeownership for low- to moderate-income buyers.

## How the Orange County DPA Program Works (Step-by-Step)

A step-by-step breakdown of how applicants move from education and lender approval to securing a home and receiving down payment assistance.

- **Complete Homebuyer Education:** Attend an approved homebuyer education course from a county-approved provider.
- **Get Mortgage Pre-Approval:** Secure a first mortgage from an approved lender (FHA, VA, USDA, or conventional, depending on eligibility).
- **Find an Eligible Home:** The home must be located in Orange County, meet the maximum purchase price limit ($345,000), and be an eligible property type.
- **Apply Through Orange County:** Submit your application through the Orange County Housing & Community Development Division.

## Key Benefits of the Orange County DPA Program

Highlights the key advantages of the program, including the amount of assistance available, forgivable loan structure, and reduced out-of-pocket costs at closing.

- Up to $70,000 in assistance for very-low-income households
- Funds can be used for down payment and closing costs
- 0% interest, deferred second mortgage
- Forgiven over 10 years (10% per year)
- Makes buying possible with minimal cash out of pocket

Loan is repaid only if you sell the home, refinance, stop using it as your primary residence, or fail to meet program terms.

## Eligibility Requirements (2026)

To qualify for the Orange County Down Payment Assistance Program, borrowers must meet all of the following:

- **First-Time Homebuyer** — No homeownership in the last 3 years.
- **Credit Score** — A credit score of 620 or higher is required.
- **Buyer Contribution** — $500 (Very Low & Low Income) and $1,000 (Moderate Income).
- **Primary Residence Only** — No rentals or second homes.
- **Eligible Properties** — Single-family homes, townhouses, condominiums, and modular homes. Mobile homes are not eligible.
- **Residency Requirement** — Applicants must have lived in Orange, Osceola, Lake, Polk, Brevard, Seminole, or Volusia County for at least the past 12 months before applying.
- **Legal Status** — Both the borrower and co-borrower must have legal permanent residency in the U.S.
