This 2026 Florida FHA eligibility guide breaks down credit score requirements, down payment rules, income verification, DTI limits, loan limits, and inspection standards, so you know exactly where you stand before applying.

## FHA Credit Score Requirements in Florida (2026)

Your credit score directly affects approval and down payment requirements. FHA credit requirements are significantly more flexible than conventional loans, which typically require 620 to 680+ scores.

**Minimum FHA Credit Scores**

- **580+ FICO** — Eligible for 3.5% down
- **500 to 579 FICO** — Eligible with 10% down
- **Below 500** — Not eligible under FHA guidelines

**If Your Credit Score Is Below 580**

You can still qualify by:

- Making a 10% down payment
- Paying down revolving debt
- Avoiding new credit inquiries
- Establishing 12 months of on-time payments

Improving your score can reduce mortgage insurance costs and improve approval odds.

## FHA Down Payment Requirements in Florida

FHA loans offer one of the lowest down payment options available.

| Credit Score | Minimum Down Payment |
|---|---|
| 580+ | 3.5% |
| 500 to 579 | 10% |

**Down Payment Flexibility**

- 100% gift funds allowed
- [Grants and down payment assistance](/learn/first-time-homebuyer/grants-and-programs) permitted
- Seller credits allowed (up to 6%)

FHA loans always require mortgage insurance, regardless of down payment size.

## FHA Loan Limits in Florida (2026)

FHA loan limits cap how much you can borrow and vary by county.

In 2026, most Florida counties follow the standard FHA one-unit loan limit of $541,287, while designated high-cost areas qualify for higher limits — reaching up to $990,150 in Monroe County and approximately $667,000 in Broward, Miami-Dade, and Palm Beach counties. See the full [FHA loan limits by county](/learn/florida-fha-loan-limits) for your area.

If a home exceeds your county limit, you must:

- Increase your down payment
- Negotiate the price
- Use a conventional or jumbo loan

## FHA Debt-to-Income (DTI) Requirements

DTI measures how much of your income goes toward debt.

**DTI Formula:** Total Monthly Debt / Gross Monthly Income = DTI

**Standard FHA DTI Guidelines**

- Front-end DTI (housing): 31%
- Back-end DTI (total debt): 43%

**Maximum FHA DTI (With Strong Factors)**

FHA can allow DTI up to 50% if you have:

- Higher credit scores
- Cash reserves
- Minimal payment shock
- Strong income stability

## FHA Home Inspection and Appraisal Requirements

FHA loans require the home to meet minimum property standards.

**FHA Appraisal Focus Areas**

- Structural soundness
- Roof life (typically 2+ years remaining)
- Electrical, plumbing, HVAC safety
- Water intrusion and moisture
- Lead-based paint hazards
- Termite damage (critical in Florida)

**Inspection vs Appraisal**

- Appraisal: Required by FHA (value + condition)
- Inspection: Strongly recommended (buyer protection)

## FHA Income and Employment Requirements

FHA does not set minimum income limits, but lenders verify stability. Some Florida assistance programs do have [income and purchase price limits](/learn/florida-housing-income-purchase-price-limits) worth checking.

**Typical Documentation**

- Last 30 days of pay stubs
- 2 years of W-2s
- 2 years of tax returns
- Proof of bonuses, overtime, commissions

**Self-Employed Borrowers**

Must provide:

- 2 years of business tax returns
- Profit and Loss statements
- Business license (if applicable)

Non-employment income (alimony, child support, rental income) can count if consistent.

## FHA vs Conventional vs VA Eligibility (Quick Comparison)

| Feature | FHA | Conventional | VA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Best for | Lower down payment and flexible credit | Stronger credit, lower MI with good scores | Eligible veterans and active duty |
| Down payment | Often 3.5% | Can be 3%+ (varies) | Often 0% |
| Credit flexibility | More flexible | Less flexible | Flexible, but lender-dependent |
| Mortgage insurance | FHA MIP required in most cases | PMI if under 20% down | No monthly MI (funding fee may apply) |
| Property standards | FHA minimum property standards | Generally less strict | VA appraisal focuses on safety and condition |
| Income limits | None | None | None |
