FHA loan limits determine how much you can borrow with an FHA mortgage based on your county and property type, and they change every year as home prices rise and fall.

For many Florida buyers — especially first-time homebuyers — these limits can be the difference between qualifying for a home or having to explore other loan options.

## 2026 FHA Loan Limits in Florida by County

Florida FHA loan limits set the maximum FHA-insured mortgage you can get in each county.

In 2026, most Florida counties use the standard 1-unit limit of $541,287, while higher-cost areas go up to $990,150 in Monroe County (and $667,000 in Broward, Miami-Dade, and Palm Beach).

FHA loan limits are set annually by HUD and vary by county based on local home prices. They determine the maximum loan amount FHA will insure, regardless of down payment size.

FHA loan limits apply to the loan amount, not the purchase price.

## How FHA Loan Limits Affect First-Time Homebuyers in Florida

FHA loans remain one of the most popular options for first-time buyers due to:

- 3.5% down payment
- Flexible credit requirements
- Higher debt-to-income allowances

However, FHA loan limits still cap borrowing power — especially in higher-priced counties.

**First-time buyers should:**

- Confirm county limits early
- Compare FHA vs conventional options
- Pair FHA with down payment assistance when possible

## How FHA Loan Limits Impact Refinancing in Florida

FHA loan limits also apply to:

- FHA rate-and-term refinances
- FHA cash-out refinances

If your loan balance exceeds current limits, you may need:

- A conventional refinance
- A non-QM or jumbo alternative

Rising home values can make refinancing more flexible — but only if limits allow.
