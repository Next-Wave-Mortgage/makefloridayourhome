A conventional mortgage is a home loan that is not insured or guaranteed by the federal government. These loans are backed by private lenders and typically follow guidelines set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac.

For Florida homebuyers with good credit and stable income, conventional loans often offer the best long-term value — lower mortgage insurance costs, competitive rates, and more property flexibility than FHA or VA loans.

## Conventional Loan Eligibility Requirements in Florida

To qualify for a conventional mortgage in Florida, you generally need:

- **Credit Score:** Minimum 620 required; higher scores unlock better rates and lower PMI costs
- **Income and Employment:** Stable, verifiable income with preferably two years of consistent work history
- **Debt-to-Income Ratio:** Generally should remain below 45%
- **Assets and Reserves:** Sufficient funds for down payment, closing costs, and reserves

## Down Payment Options for Conventional Loans

Contrary to common misconceptions, conventional loans do not always require 20% down. Florida buyers can qualify with:

- 3% down (first-time homebuyers)
- 5% down (repeat buyers)
- 10 to 20% down (stronger pricing, reduced PMI)

The 20% down payment threshold is significant because it eliminates the need for private mortgage insurance (PMI), but it is not required to qualify. Use our [home affordability calculator](/home-affordability-calculator) to estimate your monthly payment at different down payment levels.

## Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

PMI is required when putting down less than 20%. A significant advantage of conventional loans: PMI is removable once the loan balance reaches 80% of home value and must be automatically canceled at 78%.

This is a major benefit over FHA loans, where mortgage insurance often lasts the life of the loan.

## Conventional Loan Limits in Florida (2026)

For most Florida counties, the [conforming loan limit](/learn/florida-conforming-loan-limits-by-county) is $832,750 for single-unit properties, with Monroe County allowing up to $990,150. Loans exceeding these limits become jumbo conventional loans with stricter requirements.

## Conventional vs FHA Loans in Florida

**Conventional loans typically offer:**

- Lower long-term mortgage insurance costs
- Better pricing for strong-credit borrowers
- Investment property flexibility
- Removable PMI

**[FHA loans](/learn/fha-loan-eligibility-requirements-florida) may suit:**

- Lower credit scores (580+)
- Smaller down payment savings (3.5%)
- More flexible underwriting

Neither is universally superior — conventional suits strong-credit borrowers, while FHA accommodates lower credit scores and smaller down payments.

## Special Property Considerations in Florida

Conventional loans often provide more flexibility than government-backed programs for Florida condominiums, though lenders review HOA financial stability, insurance coverage, and occupancy ratios.

Conventional loans can also be used for:

- Primary residences
- Second homes
- Investment properties

This flexibility makes them popular with Florida buyers who want to own rental or vacation properties.

## Refinancing Options

Florida homeowners frequently refinance conventional mortgages to:

- Lower interest rates
- Reduce monthly payments
- Eliminate PMI
- Switch from FHA loans to save on mortgage insurance
