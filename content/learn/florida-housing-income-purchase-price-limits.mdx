Florida Housing Finance Corporation published its current 2026 limit update on June 10, 2026. The limits below apply to new Florida Housing reservations made on or after May 6, 2026.

For most buyers, this page helps answer two questions:

- Is my income under the Florida Housing limit for my county?
- Is the home price or loan amount within the program limit?

If you are comparing assistance options, start with the [Florida down payment assistance guide](/down-payment-assistance), the [Florida DPA calculator](/florida-down-payment-assistance-calculator), or the [first-time homebuyer grants guide](/learn/first-time-homebuyer/grants-and-programs).

## Start here

Before using the county table, confirm four things:

1. **County:** Use the county where the home is located, not where you live now.
2. **Household size:** Many Florida Housing limits change at three or more people.
3. **Program type:** Bond, TBA, FL Assist, FL HLP, and PLUS do not always use the same limits.
4. **Targeted area status:** Some census tracts may allow higher limits, but the property address must qualify.

[Hometown Heroes](/learn/florida-hometown-heroes-income-limits) is separate. Do not use the Standard Bond, Standard TBA, or PLUS limits on this page for a Hometown Heroes file.

## Which numbers matter

The quickest way to avoid using the wrong number is to match the limit to the loan your lender is reserving.

| If the lender is using...                                                     | The borrower usually needs to check...                   |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Standard Bond with [FL Assist](/learn/florida-assist-second-mortgage-program) | Bond income limit and Bond purchase price cap            |
| Standard Bond with FL HLP                                                     | Bond income limit and Bond purchase price cap            |
| Standard TBA with FL Assist                                                   | TBA income limit and FHA, conventional, or VA loan limit |
| Standard TBA with FL HLP                                                      | TBA income limit and FHA, conventional, or VA loan limit |
| PLUS TBA HFA Advantage                                                        | PLUS income limit and conventional or VA loan limit      |

Bond programs use **household income**, which can include more than just the people on the loan. TBA and PLUS programs use **credit income**, which is closer to the qualifying income on the mortgage application.

## How to read the table

The Bond columns are most useful when the buyer is using a Florida Housing Bond first mortgage with FL Assist. Bond files also have a purchase price cap because the program limits how much the home can cost.

The TBA columns are most useful when the buyer is using a Standard TBA first mortgage with FL Assist. TBA files are tied to loan limits instead of purchase price caps, so the FHA, conventional, or VA loan amount matters.

FL HLP and PLUS have their own exact tables. Use those below when the assistance option is not FL Assist.

## Targeted vs non-targeted areas

Most Florida homes are in non-targeted areas. Targeted areas are specific census tracts where Florida Housing may allow higher income or price limits and may also relax the first-time buyer requirement.

Do not guess based on county alone. One county can have both targeted and non-targeted tracts. The lender should verify the property address before relying on a targeted limit.

## Programs covered

These limits apply to Florida Housing Standard Bond, Standard TBA, and PLUS TBA programs. They are commonly used with FL Assist, FL HLP, and PLUS assistance.

FL Assist provides $10,000 as a 0% deferred second mortgage. FL HLP provides $12,500 as a repayable second mortgage. PLUS provides 3%, 4%, or 5% of the loan amount as a forgivable second mortgage when paired with the eligible first mortgage.

If the home is manufactured housing, also review the [Florida manufactured home loan guide](/learn/florida-manufactured-home-loan-program) because property rules can be stricter than the county income and price limits.

## Hometown Heroes

Hometown Heroes is not using these Standard Bond, Standard TBA, or PLUS limits. Use the separate [Hometown Heroes income limits guide](/learn/florida-hometown-heroes-income-limits) for that program.

For a broader assistance search, compare state, county, and city options in the [Florida first-time homebuyer grants guide](/learn/first-time-homebuyer/grants-and-programs), then use the [DPA eligibility checker](/check-dpa-eligibility) to narrow the list.
